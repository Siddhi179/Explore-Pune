Pune - The Cultural Capital of India

Introduction

Pune, known as the cultural capital of Maharashtra, is a city that celebrates a plethora of festivals with great zeal and enthusiasm.

This page is a visual journey through the vibrant festivals celebrated in Pune, their traditions, and the significant places where these celebrations take place.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is one of the most popular festivals in Pune.
The celebration begins with the installation of beautifully crafted Ganesh idols in homes and public pandals.

The festival lasts for ten days, culminating in the immersion of the idols in water bodies.

Traditions: People perform Aarti, sing devotional songs, and offer sweets like modaks to Lord Ganesha.

Significant Places: Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple, Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Mandal.

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated with much fervor in Pune. Homes are decorated with oil lamps, rangoli, and colorful lights. Fireworks light up the sky as families come together to celebrate.

Traditions: People clean and decorate their homes, perform Laxmi Puja, exchange sweets and gifts, and burst firecrackers.

Significant Places: Laxmi Road, Tulshibaug, Shaniwar Wada.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Pune. People smear each other with colors and enjoy traditional sweets and drinks.

Traditions: People gather to play with colors, sing and dance to traditional music, and indulge in festive foods.

Significant Places: Fergusson College Road, Koregaon Park.

Makar Sankranti

Makar Sankranti marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn. In Pune, it is celebrated with kite flying and feasts.

Traditions: People fly kites, prepare and share sweets made of sesame seeds and jaggery.

Significant Places: Sarasbaug, Parvati Hill.
